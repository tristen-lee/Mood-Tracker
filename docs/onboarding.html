Welcome to Cairn.

Cairn is your daily mental health check-in. Log how you're doing each day, spot patterns over time, and catch early warning signs before they become episodes. You can share your data with your doctor or therapist too.

Here's a quick rundown of what each field means.

The numbers

Mood Score (1–10) Rate how you're feeling overall on a scale of 1 to 10. Think of 1 as rock bottom — barely functional, everything feels impossible. A 5 is neutral, just getting through the day. A 10 is the best you've felt in a while. Don't overthink it, just go with your gut.
Hours of Sleep How many hours did you sleep last night? Sleep is one of the strongest predictors of episode risk, especially for mania.
Energy Level (0–10) How much energy do you have today? Think physical and mental both — not just whether you went to the gym.

The checkboxes

Feeling extra elevated? Unusually high, grandiose, impulsive, or wired. This tracks manic symptoms.
Feeling disconnected from reality? Hearing or seeing things, feeling like things aren't real, or like you're watching yourself from outside. This tracks psychosis.
Feeling low or empty inside? Sadness, numbness, heaviness, or hopelessness. This tracks depressive symptoms.
Mind going to dark places? Unwanted thoughts that keep showing up even when you don't want them to. This tracks intrusive thoughts.
Thoughts moving too fast? Can't slow your mind down. Ideas jumping around. Hard to focus. This tracks racing thoughts.
More irritable than usual? Short fuse, snapping, easily frustrated. Irritability can show up on both ends of the spectrum.
Pulling away from people? Isolating, avoiding texts, not wanting to be around anyone. This tracks social withdrawal.

Your mood state

After each check-in, Cairn calculates a combined score from your data and gives you a mood state for the day.

CrisisYour score suggests severe distress. Please reach out to someone you trust.
DepressedSigns of a depressive episode. Be gentle with yourself today.
StableYou're doing okay. Keep it up.
HypomanicElevated, but not fully manic. Keep an eye on your sleep.
ManicSigns of a manic episode. Consider reaching out to your care team.

One more thing — medications

If you take medications, you can add them in Settings and check them off each day during your check-in. This lets you see correlations between your meds and your mood over time.
