What is Cairn?

Cairn is a daily mental health check-in tool built for people managing bipolar disorder. Log how you're doing each day, see your patterns over time, and get early warnings when your data suggests an episode may be approaching.

The check-in fields

Mood Score (1–10)

Rate how you're feeling overall on a scale of 1 to 10. Think of 1 as rock bottom — barely functional, everything feels impossible. A 5 is neutral, just getting through the day. A 10 is the best you've felt in a while. Don't overthink it, just go with your gut.

Hours of Sleep

How many hours did you sleep last night? Sleep disruption is one of the strongest early warning signs for both manic and depressive episodes.

Energy Level (0–10)

How much energy do you have today? Consider both physical and mental energy. High energy with low sleep can be an early sign of hypomania.

Feeling extra elevated?

Check this if you're feeling unusually high, grandiose, impulsive, or wired. This tracks manic symptoms and raises your combined score.

Feeling disconnected from reality?

Check this if you're experiencing hallucinations, delusions, or a sense that things aren't real. Psychosis can occur on both ends of the mood spectrum and is factored into your score accordingly.

Feeling low or empty inside?

Check this if you're experiencing sadness, numbness, heaviness, or hopelessness. This tracks depressive symptoms and lowers your combined score.

Mind going to dark places?

Check this if you're having unwanted, intrusive thoughts. These are thoughts that show up even when you don't want them to.

Thoughts moving too fast?

Check this if your mind is racing — ideas jumping around, hard to focus, can't slow down. Racing thoughts often accompany elevated states.

More irritable than usual?

Check this if you have a shorter fuse than normal. Irritability can be a symptom on both the manic and depressive end of the spectrum.

Pulling away from people?

Check this if you're isolating, avoiding texts, or don't want to be around people. Social withdrawal is often a sign of depression.

Mood states

After each check-in, Cairn calculates a combined score from your inputs and assigns a mood state for the day.

  • Crisis — Severe distress. Please reach out to someone you trust or a crisis line.
  • Depressed — Signs of a depressive episode. Be gentle with yourself.
  • Stable — You're doing okay.
  • Hypomanic — Elevated but not fully manic. Watch your sleep closely.
  • Manic — Signs of a manic episode. Consider reaching out to your care team.

Episode warnings

After you've logged at least a week of data, Cairn starts watching for patterns that suggest an episode may be approaching. If it detects something, a warning card will appear on your dashboard. This is not a diagnosis — it's a heads-up based on your own data.

Your data

All your data is stored securely and is only visible to you. You can view your full history in My Entries and export or share it with your care team from the Analytics page.
